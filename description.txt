
# The Scenario

Your bot lives on **Discord**.

It has one job:

> Build a relationship with its owner from scratch.
>

When the bot starts, it knows **nothing** about:

- itself
- its owner
- the relationship between them

Through conversation, it should gradually:

- learn about its owner
- develop its own personality
- remember important things
- eventually reach out proactively

The experience should feel like **meeting a new person**, not filling out a survey.

---

# What You Should Build

## 1. Conversational Onboarding

When the bot first meets its owner, it should learn through **natural conversation**.

Not:

- forms
- surveys
- rigid questionnaires

During the conversation, the bot should gradually:
- pick a **name**
- develop a **personality**
- generate an **avatar**
based on the interaction.

The bot should be curious, but it should not interrogate the owner. Good conversations have pacing and reciprocity.

---

## 2. Persistent Identity

Everything the bot learns should be written to **files**.

When the bot restarts, it should remember:

- who it is
- who its owner is
- important things the owner has shared

These files act as the bot’s **brain**.

You do **not** need a database.

Simple local files are perfectly fine.

---

## 3. Proactive Behavior

The bot should occasionally **reach out on its own**.

For example:

Early stage

- curious
- reaches out more often
- still learning about the owner

Later stage

- more contextual
- less frequent
- less needy

If the owner ignores the bot, it should:

- back off
- try again later
- avoid spamming

A simple timer alone is not enough — the bot should have a **reasonable motivation** for reaching out and keep their owner engaged.

---

## 4. Memory That Feels Natural

The bot should reference past things the owner mentioned.

Good:

> “I know you like climbing!”
>

Bad:

> “According to message #7 your hobby is climbing.”
>

Memory should feel **natural and human**, not mechanical.

We are **not** looking for full transcript storage.

Show some judgment about what is worth remembering.

---

# Implementation Constraint (Important)

To keep the project small while still revealing real agent design:

Your bot should maintain **at least two persistent files**:

- one describing the **bot's identity**
- one describing the **owner / relationship**

These files should be **updated during conversation** and **read back into the bot’s context** when generating responses.

This ensures the bot's behavior evolves over time rather than relying on a single static prompt.

The structure of these files is completely up to you.

# Evaluation Criteria

## Product Taste

Does the bot feel like **a real personality**, or like a questionnaire?

---

## Conversational Judgment

Does it know when to ask questions, when to respond, and when to leave space?

---

## Proactive Logic

Is there real reasoning behind **when** and **why** the bot reaches out?

---

## Memory Design

Does it remember the right things and reference them naturally?

---

## Failure Handling

What happens if:

- the LLM fails
- the bot is ignored
- a tool fails

Does the system degrade gracefully?
